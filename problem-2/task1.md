Question 1: AI Drone Delivery Championship
A Smart City operates three AI-powered delivery drones—Drone Alpha, Drone Bravo,
and Drone Charlie—to transport emergency medical supplies to a Central Hospital.
Today, all three drones started their missions at the same time from different control
stations. After completing their deliveries, the Mission Control Center wants to evaluate
which drone performed the best based on its remaining battery level and mission
completion time.

Drone Alpha started its mission with 96% battery. Before takeoff, it activated its
camera system, consuming 6% battery. It then flew 20 kilometers to the hospital, using
2% battery per kilometer. After arriving, unloading the package consumed 4% battery.
It returned over the same distance using only 1% battery per kilometer and completed
the mission in 34 minutes.


Drone Bravo started with 98% battery. It used 8% battery for the camera system, flew
18 kilometers using 2% battery per kilometer, spent 5% battery unloading the
package, returned using 1% battery per kilometer, and completed the mission in 30
minutes.

Drone Charlie started with 94% battery. It used 7% battery for the camera system, flew
22 kilometers using 2% battery per kilometer, spent 6% battery unloading the
package, returned using 1% battery per kilometer, and completed the mission in 38
minutes.
According to the company's safety policy, 15% of the remaining battery must always
be reserved as an Emergency Battery Reserve.
Your Task
1. Calculate the remaining battery for each drone.
2. Calculate the usable battery after excluding the reserve.
3. Determine the 1st, 2nd and 3rd drone based on mission completion time.
4. Find the drone with the highest usable battery.
5. Print Mission Successful if usable battery is at least 20%; otherwise print Recharge
Required.


Programming Rules
• Use only single-value variables.
• Use arithmetic operators, comparison operators, and if-elif-else.
• Loops, Lists, Tuples, Dictionaries, Sets, and Functions are not allowed.
